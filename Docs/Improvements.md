## Robot Improvemets Made in May
We started by building the basic robot body and wheels. The first version used two medium motors for driving, one medium motor for steering, two ultrasonic sensors to avoid obstacles, and a gyro sensor to count laps. We also added small wheels on the sides to keep the robot from touching the walls, and a Pixy camera mounted on top of the rear wheels. All components were placed in standard positions to test basic functionality.

Later, we changed the design to use only one medium motor for driving while keeping the one medium motor for steering. To make the robot smaller and more balanced, we moved the EV3 brick closer to the steering motor and removed the side wheels. The EV3's orientation stayed the same turned sideways which kept the wires clear and easy to manage. We also relocated the Pixy camera to the front and connected it to a medium motor so it can now pan left and right. Finally, we updated the sensor configuration to use just one ultrasonic sensor to avoid obstacles, and we added a color sensor alongside the gyro sensor to handle lap counting and steering corrections.
