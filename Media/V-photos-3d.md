By building a 3D model of our robot in LEGO Studio 2.0, we were able to visualize how all the components would fit together. This let us experiment with different layouts before doing any physical assembly, making it much simpler to map out where to place the motors, sensors, and other parts. Checking the design in a 3D view allowed us to catch and correct errors early on, which kept the actual building process fast and organized while saving us a lot of time. Additionally, the digital model improved our teamwork since we could easily share and collaborate on the design, and it gave us a great visual aid for explaining our robot to other people.
